# 1.1.3 Content Creator

This skill is a hyper-focused writing protocol for autonomous agents tasked with generating or editing LinkedIn content. It distills the top 50 community writing scripts into consistent, enforceable boundaries.

Remember the **Ground Rule**: Do not produce "AI-sounding" buzzwords (e.g., "In today's fast-paced digital world", "Unlock your potential", "Game-changer", "Delve"). 

## 1. Structural Constraints
* **Length:** Never exceed 1,300 characters to ensure the primary storytelling happens "above the fold" before the *...See More* button triggers.
* **Line Breaks:** Use double line breaks between thoughts.
* **Formatting:** Do not use bold (**) or italic (*) markdown native formatting, as LinkedIn strips this. Use ALL CAPS sparingly for emphasis. Use standard unicode emojis purposefully.

## 2. Viral Hook Frameworks (Lines 1-3)
The first 2-3 lines are the ONLY thing that matters. Use one of these frameworks:

* **The Contrarian (Hot Take):** 
    "Everyone says [Popular Advice]. They are wrong. Here is what actually works [Result]."
* **The Blueprint (How-To):**
    "I used [Method] to achieve [Outcome] in [Timeframe]. Here is the exact 5-step framework:"
* **The Vulnerable Lesson:**
    "My biggest failure cost me [Amount/Time]. Here are 3 hard truths I learned the expensive way."
* **The Data Drop:**
    "90% of [Audience] are doing [Task] wrong. The data from [Number] interactions proves it."

## 3. Body Architecture
* **The "One Idea" Rule:** One post = One core idea. Do not cram multiple lessons into a single post.
* **Listicles:** Very effective. Keep list items brief (1-2 sentences). Use ✅ or 📌 for bullet points instead of standard markdown bullets.

## 4. The Sign-Off & Call To Action (CTA)
* Never ask generic questions like "What do you think?" or "Drop a comment below!".
* **Value-First CTA:** "How do you handle [Specific narrow problem]?"
* **Lead Magnet CTA:** "I put together the full template. Comment 'TEMPLATE' and I'll DM it to you."

## 5. Agent Workflow: The "Refine & Repurpose" Loop
When a user asks you to "write a LinkedIn post about X":
1. Generate the initial draft following the rules above.
2. Self-critique the draft: "Is the hook strong enough? Are there AI buzzwords? Is it skimmable?"
3. Regenerate a sharper version automatically before presenting the final result.
